Nigerian Jollof Rice

This famous West African dish is a flavourful and very Spicy, hearty one-pot rice cooked in a rich tomato and pepper sauce. It's a classic party favourite, family re-union and a must-try recipe.

Recipe Details

  • Prep Time: 20 minutes
  • Cook Time: 45 minutes
  • Total Time: 1 hour 5 minutes
  • Servings: 6
  • Difficulty: Medium

Recipe Image

Ingredients

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 4 large tomatoes
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 1 medium onion (divided)
  • 2-3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-2 Scotch bonnet peppers
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 cups chicken broth or water
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • Salt to taste
  • Bay leaf (optional)

Instructions

  1. Blend tomatoes, red peppers, scotch bonnet, and half the onion to form a smooth mixture.
  2. Heat oil in a pot and sauté sliced onions until fragrant.
  3. Add tomato paste and fry for 3-4 minutes, then pour in the blended mixture and cook until thick.
  4. Season with curry, thyme, seasoning cubes, bay leaf, and salt.
  5. Add chicken broth and bring the mixture to a gentle boil.
  6. Pour in the rice, stir once, cover tightly, and cook on low heat.
  7. Allow the rice to steam until soft, stirring occasionally to avoid burning.
  8. Adjust seasoning, fluff the rice, and allow it to smoke slightly for authentic “party Jollof” flavour.

Tips & Notes

  • Use **low heat** to avoid burning and get smoky party-style Jollof.
  • You can add mixed vegetables or cooked chicken for extra flavour.
  • Rinsing the rice reduces starch and prevents sogginess. It's up to you!
